Word Bolt
Game Tutorial About
Gameplay

At its core, WordBolt is a word finding game. The object of the game is to chain or link letters together to create words. Each WordBolt round begins with a 5x5 grid of letter tiles. Words can be created by linking adjacent letter tiles on the grid. Letter tiles can be linked diagonally, vertically, or horizontally, but each letter tile can only be used once in a word.

Each WordBolt round is 3 minutes. When the timer starts, players can begin to type words into the box below the grid. To submit a word, press “Enter” on the keyboard or click “Submit.” .

Submitted words will appear in a word list beside the grid. While the game is in progress, players can delete words they have already entered by clicking the button next to the word in the word list. This is helpful if a player accidentally mistypes or misspells a word, or submits a word like “ARENA” that cannot be found on the WordBolt grid. When 3 minutes have elapsed, all words in the word list will be scored, and players can review the results.

Valid Words

Legal words in WordBolt must be at least 4 letters long. Slang words or words of foreign origin are legal if they can be found in an English-language dictionary. Plural words are also legal, so both CENT and CENTS can be counted towards a player’s score. Like other word-finding games, such as Bananagrams and Scrabble, WordBolt does not count proper nouns (e.g. Ohio, Samuel, and Sony) as legal words.

Scoring

Only unique words found receive points. If more than one player enters in the same word, neither player will receive points for that word. For each word incorrectly entered (misspelled or not legal), 1 point is subtracted from the player’s score. Unique, acceptable words receive points based on their length.
